# Empowering Blind Individuals (Audio Books)
This paper proposes a new method of enabling visionless individuals by converting images and text of PDF files into audio formats, thus making them accessible to them. The proposed system incorporates the techniques of Optical Character Recognition and image captioning for extraction of text and visual information from input sources including images and PDF files. Text from images or textbook pages is extracted using the Tesseract OCR for textual data and BLIP (Bootstrapping Language-Image Pretraining) for non-textual image descriptions. Once such content is extracted, GPT-based text generation improves the explanation of the text and gives enough context to present a more meaningful understanding of material. The text is then converted into audio using the gTTS (Google Text-to-Speech) library, thus making it accessible to visually impaired individuals. This seamless workflow enables users to interact with educational and visual content through audio, thus significantly improving their ability to access and comprehend information that would otherwise be difficult to access. Applications will vary from accessible audio books to image-to-audio translation in real-time, meaning accessibility for the visually impaired users as well as others in more digital environments.
